Comments Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Disagreement is welcome. Disrespect is not. This publication exists to argue — but argument requires good faith. Read the rules before you comment.

What We Encourage

The Red Herring Fallacy is a publication built on the belief that argument, done well, matters. We want comments that engage directly with the piece — that bring evidence, counterargument, personal experience, or additional context that moves the conversation forward.

  • Engage directly with the argument in the piece
  • Bring evidence, data, or sources to support your position
  • Disagree — forcefully if necessary — without being abusive
  • Ask questions that genuinely seek to understand
  • Add context the piece may have missed

What We Will Remove

We will remove without warning any comment that:

  • Attacks, threatens, or harasses any person — writer, commenter, or subject of the piece
  • Uses racist, sexist, homophobic, transphobic, or otherwise discriminatory language
  • Spreads deliberate misinformation without evidence
  • Reveals private information about any individual without their consent
  • Is spam, promotional content, or completely off-topic
  • Exists solely to derail, provoke, or exhaust other commenters in bad faith

Repeat Violations

Users who repeatedly violate this policy will be banned from commenting. We reserve the right to remove any comment and ban any user at our sole discretion, without obligation to explain our decision.

Moderation

Comments are moderated by the editorial team. We are a small operation — moderation may not be immediate, but we take it seriously. If you see a comment that violates this policy, use the Disqus reporting function to flag it.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Use the contact form or reach us on Substack.
